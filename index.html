Joe Hisaishi

Bio

Joe Hisaishi (Birthname: Mamoru Fujisawa), born on 6th of December, 1950, is a Japanese omposer and musical director who is known for over 100 film scores and solo albums since 1981.

Since 1984, Joe Hisaishi has been associated with animator Hayao Miyazaki. He has created most of the musics in Hayao Miyazaki's animation.

Hisaishi was born in Nakano, Nagano, Japan. He started learning violin from the age of four, and found his passion in music. He began watching hundreds of movies a year with his father, who has a huge influence to his career. Hisaishi went to the Kunitachi College of Music in 1969 and major in music composition.

In 1983, Hisaishi created an ablum for Nausicaa the Vally of the Wind , and became great friends with the director of the animated film, Hayao Miyazaki. They started to work together on many future projects.

In 1986, Hisaishi and Hayao Miyazaki worked together on Laputa: Castle in the Sky, which is the first feature published under the Studio Ghibli brand. The musics featured in a gentle and faintly melancholic tone, which became a familiar trademark of most of the studio's later creations.
